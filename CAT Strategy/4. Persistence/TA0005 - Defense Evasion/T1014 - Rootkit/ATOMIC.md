<h2><a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#description-from-attck" id="user-content-description-from-attck"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/wiki/Technique/T1014" rel="nofollow">Description from ATT&amp;CK</a></h2>
<blockquote>Rootkits are programs that hide the existence of malware by intercepting (i.e., [Hooking](<a href="https://attack.mitre.org/techniques/T1179" rel="nofollow">https://attack.mitre.org/techniques/T1179</a>)) and modifying operating system API calls that supply system information. (Citation: Symantec Windows Rootkits) Rootkits or rootkit enabling functionality may reside at the user or kernel level in the operating system or lower, to include a [Hypervisor](<a href="https://attack.mitre.org/techniques/T1062" rel="nofollow">https://attack.mitre.org/techniques/T1062</a>), Master Boot Record, or the [System Firmware](<a href="https://attack.mitre.org/techniques/T1019" rel="nofollow">https://attack.mitre.org/techniques/T1019</a>). (Citation: Wikipedia Rootkit)
<p>Adversaries may use rootkits to hide the presence of programs, files, network connections, services, drivers, and other system components. Rootkits have been seen for Windows, Linux, and Mac OS X systems. (Citation: CrowdStrike Linux Rootkit) (Citation: BlackHat Mac OSX Rootkit)</p></blockquote><p></p>
