<h2><a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#description-from-attck" id="user-content-description-from-attck"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/wiki/Technique/T1126" rel="nofollow">Description from ATT&amp;CK</a></h2>
<blockquote>Windows shared drive and [Windows Admin Shares](<a href="https://attack.mitre.org/techniques/T1077" rel="nofollow">https://attack.mitre.org/techniques/T1077</a>) connections can be removed when no longer needed. [Net](<a href="https://attack.mitre.org/software/S0039" rel="nofollow">https://attack.mitre.org/software/S0039</a>) is an example utility that can be used to remove network share connections with the <code>net use \\system\share /delete</code> command. (Citation: Technet Net Use)
<p>Adversaries may remove share connections that are no longer useful in order to clean up traces of their operation.</p></blockquote><p></p>
